Project 01 · Case Study

Student Satisfaction &
Resource Allocation

A real management science problem: how does a university maximize student satisfaction within a fixed budget? Two interconnected optimization problems, one structured answer.

Project Overview

This project is based on a structured case analysis of Dong-A University and its international students. We approached it as a management science problem: define the objective, identify the constraints, enumerate solutions, and recommend the best feasible option.

The analysis covers two problems: (1) maximizing student satisfaction while managing costs, and (2) allocating production resources between two products to maximize profit.

Problem 1 — Student Satisfaction

What is Student Satisfaction?

Student satisfaction measures how happy students are with their overall university experience — education quality, facilities, campus life, and support services.

How Do We Measure It?

  • Student surveys and feedback forms
  • Student retention rates (how many continue studying)
  • Graduation rates and application numbers
  • Complaints and suggestions submitted
// Management Science Model — Problem 1
ObjectiveMaximize student satisfaction while maintaining or increasing tuition revenue and retaining international students
ConstraintsTuition cannot drop significantly (damages image + finances) · Limited university budget · Changes must be practical and sustainable
Known ProblemsHigh fees relative to value perceived · Heavy workload · English lecture quality · Limited facility hours · Insufficient support services

Possible Solutions

Option A

Improve Learning Environment

Modern classrooms, labs, better study spaces, upgraded educational technology.

Option B

Upgrade Campus Facilities

Gym equipment, extended operating hours (especially vacations), better recreational infrastructure.

Option C

Native English Professors

Hire more foreign instructors for English-taught courses, improving communication quality for international students.

✓ RECOMMENDED COMBINED

The recommended solution combines all three: improve educational quality and campus facilities rather than lowering tuition. Better classrooms, modern facilities, extended gym access, and more native English-speaking professors can significantly improve satisfaction while maintaining revenue.

What Did I Learn?

Knowledge

Structuring real problems

The hardest part of management science isn't the math — it's translating a vague real-world situation into a precise objective function with clear constraints. This case forced that discipline.

Skills

Tradeoff analysis

Neither product A-only nor product B-only is optimal. The best solution is a blend that trades some profit potential for risk reduction. That insight generalizes to almost every resource allocation problem.

Reflection

Cost vs. value

For Dong-A University, the lesson was: don't reduce price to increase satisfaction. Increase value instead. That's a management insight, not just an economic one — the constraint shapes the solution.
