Hero & Formation Setup
Do this before you enter — not during prep phase
✅ Best Practice
⏳Don't set your formation until just before you start. Activate your buffs first, then set formations so the buffs are locked in.
⚔️Set one strong attack formation, one strong defence formation, and fill the third with your next best troops.
🦸Use your best heroes generally. Expedition skills are active — avoid Diana and blue heroes if you have comparable alternatives, but don't sacrifice raw power over it.
💡 Why It Matters
⚡Things happen fast. You'll need to react without waiting for teammates, so all three formations must be ready before you enter.
🔵Each player can field 3 squads — 9 heroes total. Don't leave squads half-built.
💎Gems can reduce march time (10s → 5s). Save this for Temple phase only — wasteful in regular lane fights.
📌 Officer Checklist Before Saturday
›Name one lead caller and one backup caller.
›Assign lane groups and reaction groups before members enter.
›Pin the first three route calls in alliance chat.
›Mark Garrison priority and Temple regroup timing.
›Set up voice chat (Discord) — real-time calls are dramatically faster than typing during Garrison and Temple phases.
🏆 The Only Question That Matters

One mindset shift that fixes most bad Tri-Alliance Clash decisions.

💡
Stop Asking "Where Can I Fight?"
The one officer rule that changes everything
Ask this instead: "Which building, lane, or route gives us points later?" That single question fixes most bad TAC decisions. Kills feel productive, but building control and point timing decide most matches.
❌ Losing Habit
🚫Strongest players wander into random fights — you lose map structure even while the battle report looks active.
🚫Chasing kills instead of securing the access routes to the Temple (A29/B29/C29). Lose those and you can't even reach the Temple in Phase 4.
🚫Entering the Temple phase scattered with no shared timing.
✅ Winning Habit
🏰Every member knows their building, lane, or route assignment before the map opens.
⏱️Energy is managed — save 30–40% for the 20-minute Garrison unlock and the 40-minute Temple rush.
🗣️Caller sends short calls: objective, route, timing. Not a speech — three pieces of info maximum.
⚔️

The Short Answer

Tri-Alliance Clash is won by point control, not looking busy. A clean plan with imperfect players beats a perfect theory shouted in chat after the map opens. Write the route plan before the battle starts. Every time. ✌️

Phase 1 — Preparation (3 min)
No combat · Positioning only
✅ Use This Window
📍Confirm lane assignments — every member should know exactly where they're going before the timer hits zero.
🎙️Caller reads out the first three route objectives in voice.
🏃Position for the Transit Center rush — securing these first gives superior map mobility for the whole match.
❌ Don't
🚫Use this phase for strategy discussion — that should already be done. Prep phase is too short.
🚫Wander without an assignment. If you don't know where to go, ask now — not at 0:05.
0:03
Phase 2 — Seize & Conquer (17 min)
Outer buildings unlock · Transit Centers · Early pressure
✅ Priority
🚇Transit Centers first. These grant superior map mobility and are the only route to the Temple in Phase 4. Losing A29/B29/C29 can lock you out of the final fight entirely.
🏰Capture all assigned lane buildings. Establish a points-per-minute lead early.
⚡Steal a few smaller enemy buildings for early score and route pressure.
⚡ Energy Rule
🔋Save 30–40% energy for the 20-minute Garrison unlock. Burning out here leaves you weak when it matters most.
🚫Garrisons are shielded in this phase — don't waste marches on them yet.
👨‍✈️Assign Captains to captured buildings when possible — they regenerate energy much faster than regular players.
0:20
Phase 3 — Garrison Occupation (20 min)
Garrisons unlock · +1800 pts/min each · Mid-game swing point
✅ Priority
🏰Hold your own Garrison above all else. Garrisons give +1800 points per minute — 3x more than a standard building. Losing yours can flip the score fast.
⚔️If resources allow, raid an enemy Garrison for massive point swings.
🔀Special Forces should disrupt enemy backlines now to weaken their Temple push before Phase 4 begins.
📌 Prep for Phase 4
⛩️Begin regrouping towards Temple access routes (A29/B29/C29).
🕐Call the Temple regroup timing in alliance chat or voice before 0:35.
❤️Players who need to heal should rotate now — don't enter Phase 4 wounded.
💡 Bypass rule: You can bypass a building without capturing it if you have at least 5 squads present. Use this to push through contested routes toward the Temple without getting bogged down.
0:40
Phase 4 — Temple Onslaught (20 min)
Temple of Tides · 50,000 pt bonus · Match decider
✅ Main Force
⛩️First-capture points are huge. Time the rush so your strongest marches hit the Temple doors together — not trickling in one at a time.
🏆The alliance holding the Temple at the end gets a 50,000 point bonus — this single building usually determines the winner.
🔒Final Blow Rule: For the Temple, the alliance that lands the last hit on the final guard claims full control. Coordinate your timing.
🧠 The Centre Spam Strategy
💡Near the final minutes, spam all available marches into the Temple — each march from a single member (up to 3) takes 10 seconds of control time.
🎯Even without the biggest players, this can win you the 50k final control bonus. Risky but it has worked repeatedly.
🛡️Defence teams hold access routes (A29/B29/C29). If enemies cut those, your Temple force gets isolated.
⚠️ Do not enter Phase 4 scattered. An uncoordinated rush into the Temple is easier to repel than a unified push. Use the shared clock — call the regroup point before 0:38 and move as one.

🎯 Role Assignments

Assign roles before the battle. A smaller player with perfect discipline is more valuable than a strong player with no structure.

👥
The 4 Core Roles
Every player must know their role before Saturday
⚔️ Main Players (Lane Holders)
Capture the first keypoint in their assigned lane immediately. Hold constant frontline pressure. Do not leave just because another fight looks more exciting. Coordinate with their two Supporters for healing rotation.
🛡️ Support Players
Position behind the Main Player. Protect lane continuity — your job is to keep the point structure alive while stronger players heal or rotate. Defend against enemy pushes into your territory and recover buildings lost during lane pressure.
⚡ Reaction Team
Move ONLY when the caller sends you. A reaction team that freelances stops being a reaction team. Secure early neutral buildings in the central region. Prevent enemies from gaining early point advantages. Flexible response to wherever the map needs help most.
🗡️ Special Forces
Breakthrough unit — bypass buildings using the 5-squad rule and disrupt enemy backlines. Ideal in Phase 3 to weaken the enemy Temple push before Phase 4 starts. This is one of the strongest tactical tools in the entire event.
🎙️ Callers (R5/R4): Name one lead caller and one backup before the battle. Keep calls to three pieces of info max — objective, route, timing. Members should hear a direction, not a speech. Short calls in real-time win matches.
💡
Role Tips by Player Size
Every player has a useful role regardless of power
🏆 Strong Players
⚔️Main lane holders and first Garrison assault.
⛩️Lead the Temple rush in Phase 4.
🎯Special Forces breakthrough if coordination allows.
🌱 Smaller Players
📞Caller or backup caller — discipline matters more than power here.
🛡️Support lanes and handle timing calls.
🕐Keep route structures stable and buildings ticking over while main players fight.
💡 Players who stay alive and rotate cleanly create more value than players who dive until they're forced back to base. Assign support players to stabilize lanes and give main players time to heal without losing the anchor point.
⚡
Energy Management
The hidden win condition most alliances ignore
✅ Energy Rules
🔋Save 30–40% energy going into the 0:20 Garrison phase. Arriving empty means missing the most valuable buildings in the match.
👨‍✈️Assign Captains to key buildings after capturing them — Captains regenerate energy far faster than normal players.
💎Gems can halve march time (10s → 5s). Only worth using in the Temple phase — not regular skirmishes.
❌ Energy Mistakes
🚫Burning full energy in Phase 2 chasing kills — arriving at Garrisons with nothing left.
🚫Leaving buildings uncaptained — you lose the energy regen advantage.
🚫Using gems on random lane fights — save them for when it actually changes the match outcome.
