Final Reflection

What this course changed

Most Interesting

The Gravity Model

I didn't expect a physics equation to work so cleanly for trade. The idea that geography still dominates even in the internet age was genuinely surprising — and it made me rethink how "global" our economy really is.

Most Challenging

Defending Free Trade

The DAH Snarky debate forced me to argue a position under pressure. Autarky made more sense than I expected at first — finding the right counter-arguments pushed me to actually understand the theory, not just repeat it.

Most Valuable

Boots Theory

This concept left the classroom. I now use it constantly: when buying a bag, choosing a laptop, evaluating suppliers. The idea that cheap can be expensive is simple but powerful, and it connects microeconomics to real-world inequality.

How My Understanding Changed

Trade has a human face

From the story of a trench trade to the degrowth critique, I learned that trade theory isn't abstract. It's what happens when a dying soldier trades chocolate for an apple — and both feel richer afterward.
