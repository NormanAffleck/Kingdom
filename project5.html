Carrier Bag, Degrowth
& the GVCs

Three readings that challenge the core assumptions behind globalization — about who builds civilization, whether growth can be endless, and what global production really costs the planet.

Project Overview

What are these readings about?

Reading 5 brought together three texts with a common thread: they all challenge dominant assumptions. Ursula Le Guin's Carrier Bag Theory challenges who built civilization. The Degrowth reading challenges whether growth is always desirable. The GVC reading challenges whether globalization automatically benefits everyone without environmental cost.

One-sentence summary for the exam: Sustainable development requires rethinking assumptions about human progress, economic growth, and global production by emphasizing cooperation, ecological limits, and environmentally responsible value chains.

Three Ideas That Challenge the Default

🛍️

Carrier Bag Theory

Civilization wasn't built by heroes with weapons — it was built by people who gathered, stored, and shared. The bag, not the spear.

🌱

Degrowth

Planned reduction of material throughput — not recession, not crisis, but a deliberate choice to consume less while improving wellbeing and equality.

🌐

Global Value Chains

Production spread across countries creates efficiency — and environmental costs, carbon leakage, and inequality that markets don't automatically fix.

Understanding Throughput

Key Vocabulary

Carrier Bag Theory
Civilization depends on cooperation and gathering, not only on heroes and conquest
Throughput
Flow of resources through the economy: Nature → Production → Consumption → Waste
Degrowth
Planned reduction of material and energy use to reduce ecological damage
Carbon Leakage
When strict environmental policy causes production to move to looser-rules countries
CBAM
Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism — tariff on imports based on carbon intensity
ETS
Emissions Trading System — cap-and-trade policy for carbon emissions
GVC
Global Value Chain — production process spread across multiple countries
Green Growth
Keeping economic growth while reducing environmental impact — degrowth's rival idea

What Did I Learn?

Knowledge

Growth has limits

I understood environmentalism before this course. I didn't understand it connects directly to trade theory — that carbon leakage is a market failure and GVCs are part of the emissions problem.

Skills

Reading critically

These texts required asking "what assumption is this challenging?" That critical stance toward dominant ideas is a skill I'll use well beyond this course.

Reflection

The Le Guin surprise

I didn't expect a science fiction author in a business course. But Carrier Bag Theory was one of the most useful reframings of the semester — who gets credited, and whose work goes unnoticed?
