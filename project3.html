Project Overview

What is Boots Theory?

This project features a class presentation on Boots Theory — a concept from Terry Pratchett's novel that reveals a profound economic truth: it is expensive to be poor.

A poor person buys $10 boots that last one year. Over 10 years: $100 spent. A wealthy person buys $50 boots that last 10 years. After the same period: $50 spent. The poor person spent twice as much — because they couldn't afford to buy cheap.

The presentation below explores how this scales from boots to fast fashion, housing, healthcare, and global supply chains.

What Did I Learn?

Knowledge

Long-run vs. short-run costs

Economics distinguishes short-run and long-run thinking. Boots Theory is a lived illustration of why optimizing for today can be irrational tomorrow — especially for people with budget constraints.

Skills

Communicating economics

Presenting this concept required translating dry economic logic into something a general audience could feel. Terry Pratchett did it better than any textbook.

Reflection

Poverty is structural

Boots Theory shifts blame away from individuals. It's not that poor people make worse decisions — it's that financial barriers make the rational long-run choice inaccessible. That has deep policy implications.
